The Numbers

A $31.7 billion industry.
Built by artists. Owned by someone else.

Recorded music just had its 11th consecutive year of growth. Music catalogs sell for 12–18 times their annual earnings. Music royalties have historically returned 13% annually, largely uncorrelated with the stock market. This is one of the best-performing asset classes on the planet. It has never been available to the people who actually built it.

For Artists

One single. One year.
One event that changes everything.

SoundSteak is a record label built differently from the ground up. The contract it offers is scoped to a single release — not an album deal, not a multi-year lock-in, not a vague promise. The journey is tailored to where you are and where you want to go. Every registration, every split, every legal structure is in place before you record a note. And when you receive your recording contract with SoundSteak, the world gets its first chance to own a piece of what you’re about to make.

This record label does what is in the best interest of the artist — because that is what is in the best interest of the SteakHolders, and in turn, SoundSteak. That alignment is not a policy. It is the structure.

1
Apply
Artists apply each cycle. To advance, you record a verified live video — the platform confirms it’s real, in real time. Then fans pledge $1 each to back you. The pledges cover pre-signing costs and prove there are real people behind the music. The label doesn’t pick the winners. The people do.

The gong rings. The contract is awarded and the IPO happens in the same moment — ownership of the release opens to SteakHolders for the first time. You show up to make music. The business is already built.

Funding doesn’t trickle in — it has a target and a deadline. The raise must hit its goal within a defined window or recording doesn’t begin. When it clears, the clock starts immediately. This is not a slow label. The speed is by design — momentum is part of the release strategy, and investors know exactly when the work begins.
4
The Year — Recording to Release
Twelve months from threshold to drop. Recording, production, campaign — milestone-funded and moving. Capital releases as verified work gets done, not all at once. Every step tracked on the artist’s profile, visible to every SteakHolder in real time. The goal is a finished single ready to release at the next event.
5
Event Two — The Release
One year later, the gong rings again. The single drops publicly at the next event. Earnings begin flowing immediately — tracked in real time across streams, sync, and every other revenue source. The artist’s profile becomes a live performance record, charted like a stock. The music stays in the system, earning and available for new investment, until the artist initiates the exit.
